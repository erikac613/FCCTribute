Linda Schele:

Expert in Mayan Epigraphy and Iconography

Here is a timeline of Dr. Schele's life:

  • 1942: Born in Nashville, TN
  • 1964: Earned Bachelor's Degree in Education and Art from the University of Cincinnati
  • 1968: Earned Master's Degree in Art from the University of Cincinnati; started teaching at the University of South Alabama
  • 1970: Traveled to Yucatan to photograph Mesoamerican monuments for the University of South Alabama
  • 1971: Returned to Yucatan to draw and study Mesoamerican architecture and art, and became interested in the culture and history of the indigenous people of the region
  • 1973: Deciphered a section of the list of Palenque Kings alongside Peter Mathews, establishing Schele in Mayan studies not just in art and history, but also epigraphy and archaeology
  • 1975: Became a Fellow in Pre-Columbian studies at Dumbarton Oaks, Washington, D.C.
  • 1977: Founded Mayan Heiroglyph Workshop in Texas
  • 1980: Earned PhD in Latin American Studies at the University of Texas
  • 1986: Schele and associates identified the dynastic founder of Copan, Yax Kuk Mo, and presented groundbreaking evidence at a Mayan art exhibition, "The Blood of Kings: A New Interpretation of Maya Art," one of he most influential studies of the Maya and their ancient culture to date
  • 1988: Organized 13 workshops on heiroglyphic writing in Guatemala and Mexico
  • 1998: Linda Schele passed away from pancreatic cancer

You can learn more about Dr. Schele and her amazing contributions to archaeology on her Wikipedia page or at the University of Texas Digital Repository.
